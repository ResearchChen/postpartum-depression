  • Common Concerns

  • MYTH: Postpartum depression always occurs in the first few months following childbirth. FACT: It can occur at any point during 1st year following childbirth.

  • MYTH: People with postpartum depression cry all the time. FACT: Postpartum depression symptoms vary from person to person. Some people hold their feelings inside. A person who looks okay does not necessarily mean they are really okay!

  • MYTH: Postpartum depression means a person does not love their baby. FACT: Postpartum depression does not represent love or hate. It is linked with chemical imbalances, hormonal fluctuations, and other factors!

  • MYTH: Postpartum depression will fade away on its own. FACT: Postpartum depression does not go away on its own. However, it is extremely treatable and responds well to psychotherapy and medication interventions.

How can I help?

  • You can help by knowing the signs of depressive symptoms and urging your loved one to seek medical care.
  • Be a good listener. Let them know you are there for them and you care for them.
  • Offer to help them with daily tasks like cleaning and running errands.
  • Encourage them to seek help from mental health professionals. Offer to book an appointment for them or let them know you are willing to go with them to provide support.

My partner gave birth, but I also feel depressed. Can the other parent get postpartum depression as well?

  • Short answer: Yes! Both parents can experience depressive symptoms.
  • 1 in 10 men encounters postpartum depression, a prevalence that is noteworthy when compared to the 1 in 7 women who also experience this challenge.
  • An estimated 4% of parents experience depression during 1st year after their childbirth.

I feel better now! But I am worried, will the disorder come back again?

  • If you have postpartum depression in one pregnancy, there is a minimum 25% chance of having a non-childbirth-related episode of the disorder and a maximum 40% chance of having another postpartum episode in another pregnancy.
  • All recurrences have an approximate 24% chance of occurring within the first two weeks postpartum.

Do not hesitate to seek support. It's perfectly acceptable to express when you are not feeling okay. Reaching out for help is a courageous step towards healing, and acknowledging your emotions is the first step on the path to well-being.
