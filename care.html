  • Self-Care

  • Postpartum depressive symptoms do not include hearing voices or hallucinating. If you are feeling manic or paranoid and hallucinating, then you might be experiencing postpartum psychosis or other mental disorders.

  • Postpartum depression does not cause people to harm their babies. But people may have suicidal ideation. Call your doctor or the suicide crisis helpline (988) if you are experiencing emotional distress.

  • The demands of caring for a newborn may make it challenging to recognize your own depression. The changes in your behavior and mood can be ignored and overlooked. It is not selfish to make yourself a priority!

  • It is time to re-examine your schedule! It should not brim with activities for your babies but lack any of your own.

  • +
    Schedule visits with your friends and family. Make calls with your friends and family more often.
    +
    Isolation can make depression worse and strong social bonding can help you to reduce the stress of caring for a newborn.
    +
    Ask for help with preparing food and doing other daily tasks. Let others help you and share responsibilities. Family and friends are often happy to help a mother with a newborn.
  • +
    Keep your shades and curtains open and go outside as much as possible. Sunlight can help promote a positive mood and emotion. Sunlight can help to alleviate depressive symptoms faster than antidepressants.
    +
    Exercise such as outdoor stroller walks can help to improve your mood.
    +
    Do not overdo and remember to have as much rest and sleep as possible. Fatigue can increase depression.
  • +
    Avoid alcohol and caffeine. No need to eat excessive food when you are not feeling hungry. Eat small snacks throughout the day.
    +
    Join a support group of families with new babies.
    +
    Play upbeat music in the daytime and soothing music in the evening.
